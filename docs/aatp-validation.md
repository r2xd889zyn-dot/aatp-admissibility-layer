# AATP Validation Framework

## Purpose

This document establishes how AATP is validated as a structural system.

Validation is not based on performance metrics, but on structural recurrence and failure-mode explanation across domains.

---

## Core Invariant

A system’s behavior is determined not only by how it processes information,  
but by what information it is allowed to process.

---

## Validation Method

### 1. Cross-Domain Recurrence

The same structural pattern appears across multiple domains:

- AI systems → hallucination and drift from unvalidated context  
- Governance systems → compliant but harmful outcomes  
- Economic systems → rational decisions leading to structural collapse  
- Autonomous systems → coherent behavior misaligned with reality  

This recurrence demonstrates that the problem is structural, not domain-specific.

---

### 2. Failure Mode

Systems can remain:

- coherent  
- consistent  
- fully operational  

While still producing incorrect or harmful outcomes.

This occurs when:

→ invalid or unbounded inputs are admitted into the system

---

### 3. Intervention Point

Traditional systems act at:

- output  
- execution  
- monitoring  

AATP introduces control at:

→ signal admission and admissibility  

before context formation occurs.

---

## Conclusion

AATP is validated by demonstrating that:

1. The same structural failure appears across domains  
2. The failure originates at signal admission  
3. Controlling admissibility changes system outcomes upstream  

---

## Final Statement

Systems do not fail at execution.

They fail at what they were allowed to become.
